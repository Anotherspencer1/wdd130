Overview

Purpose

[I'm a software engineer, but I love to garden and create a lush paradise for myself and others to enjoy. Over the years I have found myself struggling to find the right plants to survive the local climate, pests, and diseases. I know so many others have had similar issues. With my experience in software and gardening I can create a website to house lists regarding the best plants to grow in each climate, with detailed information about how to care and protect each plant. This can be a centralized site where anyone, novice, or expert, can learn what they need to garden in confidence. Would a resource like this boost your confidence in your ability to garden?]

Audience

[My target audience is anyone that gardens. This site will most likely attract those who are just getting into the hobby, so I anticipate a lot of new home owners. Therefore, I anticipate that a large majority of the site users will be between 20 to 30. Due to the higher percentage of younger people, this site needs to be optimized for smaller screens so it can be easily accessed via phone. I do however, expect some older people with more experience. The site will be well laid out in order to accommadate those without much internet familiarity.]

Content

Home page

Site Intentions This website aims to be friendly and useful for beginning to intermediate level gardeners. These gardeners will be looking for easy to find and useful information to get started in their garden. Therefore, the website will contain a home page with all the necessary links and some example images and texts related to different zones and common plants. Another page will be devoted to the different zones, and the top 5 most common plants for each respective zone. Locations and climates will be listed to aid the user in finding their own respective zone. Lastly, a third page will be devoted to a quick start guide and common dos and don'ts of gardening. It will outline common mistakes and oversights when designing and maintaining a garden. Main Page The primary page of the website will be dedicated to guiding the user in the use of the website. It will provide some background of the website, as well as instruct the user on how to find what they are looking for. It will aim to be inviting for the user through the use of beautiful imagery and garden goals. Some motivation will be found, including the benefits of managing your own garden and some testimonials of the joy and fun it can be. This page will also include all the links to the other two pages of the site.

Getting Started

Second Page: Getting Started This page will be a quick start guide for the user. This page is aimed primarily at someone who has little confidence in their knowledge, or who has never gardened before. The page will consist of the most common tips and tricks as well as pitfalls in order to guide the user to a successful garden. Some information including will be pros and cons of a raised bed vs in ground, pesticides, treatment for common pests, necessary tools, and expected costs. The content will be organized from most commonly known, to least commonly known. The goal being; the user gains confidence through knowing how to avoid common struggle points. This page will also outline differences between growing edibles, or decorative plants.

Images for the Page 2

Hardiness Zone Guide

Third Page: Zones and Plants This page is for anyone trying to plan out their garden. They will have hopefully already read the quick start page and are ready to start planting. In gardening we have different zone numbers assigned to the various different climate types in the world. Knowing what zone you live in is essential to picking the right plants and knowing how to care for your garden. The “hardiness zone” is based primarily on the lowest and highest temperatures of each area, and it dictates how well each species of plant will grow in said area. Therefore, this page will be crucial for anyone planning to start a garden. This page will have each zone listed with its respective climate type in order for the user to identify their zone. Once identified the zone description will be followed be a list of commonly strong plants in that zone. This can be an extremely quick and easy way for the user to pick some plants for their garden, or it will serve as a strong guide for the user to know how to find their own plants that fit well with their zone. Along with the zones and plants will be some tips on how to manage your garden to create a microclimate, or change the zone you live in. For example, a greenhouse will raise the lowest temperature of an area dramatically, creating a microclimate that is possible 2 or 3 zones higher than the actual zone of the area. There are several things that can be done with this same effect.
